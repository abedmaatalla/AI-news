Get a bird’s-eye view of Norway, literally, in this eight-day journey to Norway with the noted drone photographer Josh Haner. Spend a week exploring above the Arctic Circle, where darkness never truly falls during the summer. Learn to fly a drone, or perfect your skills, and capture your own videos and imagery of wild landscapes, arctic wildlife and the locals who call this wondrous place home.

Josh Haner is a Pulitzer Prize-winning photographer who has revolutionized the use of drones for journalism, and you can learn some of his tips and techniques. Each day will include one or two drone photography sessions where you can learn the basics or become more practiced. In addition, learn what makes this place special as you visit an ancient Viking settlement, stop for a Viking lunch, hike a local summit, and stroll the art galleries of the picturesque Lofoten Islands. Visit places few people go with and capture views even fewer get with Times Journeys.

Your journey includes:

  • On Tour Team: New York Times Expert, professional tour leader and local guides
  • Transfers: Private arrival and departure transfers based on flight arrival and departure times
  • Food & Drink: Most meals, plus welcome and farewell receptions. Bottled water throughout.
  • Accommodations: at 4- or 5- star hotels, including wifi where available
  • Logistics: Gratuities and taxes
  • Activities/Fees: Included for all per itinerary
  • Drones will be provided to the passengers who do not bring their own

Day 1

Welcome to Norway

Arrive at Tromso Airport, and transfer to your hotel. Because Tromso is on the Gulf Stream, it is often warmer than the surrounding areas. Meet your fellow Times Journeys travelers, guides and expert for a trip briefing, and then walk to a local restaurant for a welcome dinner. After dinner, if weather permits, you can start your drone flying under the midnight sun.

Day 2

The Sami People and Their Reindeer

After breakfast, prepare for a full day exploring the area around Tromso. Today’s theme will be Arctic wildlife and the Sami people, nomads who travel with herds of reindeer, which provide them with food, fur and transportation. (You may know them as Laplanders.) Lunch will be outdoors, weather permitting. After dinner hope for good weather, and do some more filming in the Arctic twilight.

Day 3

The Lofoten Islands

Take a short flight from Tromso to Svolvaer, arriving early enough to allow for almost a full day of photography. The Lofoten Islands are famous for steep mountains, clear water with sandy beaches and some of the world’s best cod fishing. It takes only two hours from one side of the island to the other, so there will be plenty of time to explore and find the best spots for pictures and drone flying.

Day 4

Surfing

Drive toward the end of the Lofoten Islands. Stop at the Lofotr Viking Museum, which includes the largest Viking house found to date, and have a Viking lunch in one of the houses. Continue to Unstad Beach, which draws surfers from around the world and which was featured in a Times article in 2016. Though there are only 15 residents here, the population swells when the waves do. Even if there are no surfers, the mountainous backdrop and the profusion of seabirds make for great photography. Capture your own video or images, and, using the storytelling tools you have gained, discuss them in the context of that 2016 article. Return to Henningsvaer for some free time before dinner.

Day 5

Villages and Views

Explore the area around Henningsvaer and the villages of Kabelvag and Kalle. Hike to one of the summits to capture the great view. (You can divide into groups for different hikes for difficulty and preference.) Later, spend more time photographing the sandy beaches and clear blue water of the harbor, and visiting the local art galleries.

Day 6

Whales and Wildlife

Drive to Senja, with wonderfully diverse scenery and wildlife. Along the way, stop in Andenes, an old fishing village where you are likely to see many different whales and other Arctic wildlife. Continue by ferry to Senja, the country’s second-largest island, whose outline reflects Norway’s jagged coasts.

Day 7

Senja Island

Take most of the morning to explore and photograph Senja. With broad coastal plains, jagged peaks and dense forests, the island does not lack for scenic views. This afternoon return to Tromso, stopping for some final filming opportunities. Tonight have a farewell dinner in the city center.

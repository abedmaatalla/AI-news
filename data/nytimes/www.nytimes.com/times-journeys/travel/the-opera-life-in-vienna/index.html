Vienna is the world capital of classical music and has a superb opera house that every opera lover dreams of attending. The city also has amazing art collections, fabulous food and wine, and great shopping. Join Fred Plotkin, an opera expert and Vienna regular for 43 years, on an unforgettable eight-day adventure, including four performances at the State Opera.

On this extraordinary itinerary, you will attend four operas: “Tosca,” “Aïda,” “L’Elisir d’Amore” and the premiere of a new production of “Otello,” starring some of the world’s leading singers. A full-day excursion takes you to Eisenstadt, where Haydn lived and worked. Immerse yourself in the music you love, in the city that loves it, too. With plenty of free time to enjoy all that Vienna has to offer and Fred Plotkin’s insight, this is an opera lover’s ultimate experience.

Scheduled Experts

  • Fred Plotkin

    Jun 14–21 departure
    Fred Plotkin is one of the world’s most respected opera experts and author of “Opera 101: A Complete Guide to Learning and Loving Opera.” He has lectured, taught, consulted and held managerial or artistic roles at some of the top opera companies, including La Scala and the Metropolitan Opera. He is a frequent visitor to Italy, Germany, Austria, France and Britain and has immersed himself in these countries' rich cultures.

Journey Highlights

  • Take a day trip to Eisenstadt, where Haydn lived and composed.

Day 2

All-Star “Tosca” at the State Opera

After breakfast and over lunch, Fred Plotkin will discuss Viennese history, culture and the city’s role in opera. Tonight, attend Puccini’s “Tosca” at the Vienna State Opera, with Marco Armiliato scheduled to conduct a stellar cast that is to include Nina Stemme, Piotr Beczala and Carlos Álvarez. Nicola Benois’s classic set and costume design allow you to be transported to 19th-century Rome. Discover one of Vienna’s famous restaurants for dinner on your own.

Day 3

Museum Exploration and Free Time in Vienna

Take the day to explore Vienna. This city has some of the greatest museums in the world. There is the Kunsthistorisches Museum, the museum of art history, with its stupendous collection of old masters. The Belvedere has Klimt and Schiele masterpieces to match its Baroque setting. The Museum of Applied Arts is a shrine to Jugendstil, or Art Nouveau, and the Wiener Werkstätte. Or maintain your music theme and discover the homes of Mozart, Beethoven and Schubert, or take in a concert or church service with music. Lunch and dinner are times to explore Vienna’s dining scene.

Day 4

Winery Lunch and Donizetti’s “L’Elisir d’Amore”

With nearly three square miles of grapes in the city limits, Vienna produces more wine than any world capital. Take a streetcar to the wine-producing district for lunch in one of the wineries and taste the wines produced there. In the evening, attend Donizetti’s “L’Elisir d’Amore” at the Vienna State Opera, scheduled to star Andrea Carroll, Saimir Pirgu, Samuel Hasselhorn and Ambrogio Maestri. (The “Elixir of Love” is, of course, wine.) Dinner is on your own

Day 5

Haydn in Eisenstadt

Franz Josef Haydn (1732–1809) is the father of the symphony and the string quartet and composer of some 20 operas. He worked under the enlightened patronage of Nikolaus I, Prince Esterházy in Eisenstadt, 30 miles east of Vienna. On a day trip, visit Haydn’s home and church and have lunch. If there is a concert in the Haydnsaal of the Esterházy Palace, attend it before returning to Vienna for an evening and dinner on your own.

Day 6

Discovering Sigmund Freud in “Aïda”

Join Fred Plotkin for a talk on Sigmund Freud’s influence on operas such as Richard Strauss’s “Salome” and “Elektra.” Then visit the museum dedicated to Freud. Have lunch together at a local restaurant. Tonight, attend a performance of Verdi’s “Aïda” at the Vienna State Opera. Marco Armiliato is scheduled to conduct a cast that is to include Ekaterina Gubanova, Elena Guseva, Gregory Kunde and Simone Piazzola. Dinner is on your own.

Day 7

Farewell Luncheon and Verdi’s “Otello”

Spend much of the day exploring more of Vienna, stopping for a farewell lunch with your new friends. In the evening, attend the premiere of a new production of Verdi’s thrilling “Otello” at the Vienna State Opera. Stage directed by Adrian Noble, it is scheduled to star Aleksandrs Antonenko, Olga Bezsmertna and Vladislav Sulimsky and be conducted by Myung-Whun Chung. This stunning production is a great way to end your opera experience. Relax with dinner on your own.

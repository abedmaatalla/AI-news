Couture Week, in Pictures

Jan. 22, 2019

The spring 2019 haute couture collections have glided across Paris’s runways, bringing the twice-yearly festivities to a close. Here, our favorite images from the shows as captured by T’s photographers.

Wednesday, Jan. 23

On the last day of couture week, Pierpaolo Piccioli of Valentino presented a collection that reportedly left Celine Dion in tears. Jewel-toned separates prefaced a parade of floral gowns — some richly printed, others cut from lace, embroidered, spangled or fringed. The finishing touch on a handful of the 65 looks came in the form of feather eyelash extensions, affixed by the makeup artist Pat McGrath.

Tuesday, Jan. 22

Filing through the majestic ’30s-era galleries of the Musée d’Art Moderne de la Ville de Paris, the models at Givenchy wore looks from a collection that Clare Waight Keller described as an ode to the natural world. The pieces emphasized both lightness — with delicate silver-flecked lace and a palette of mainly black and white — as well as technical intricacy, manifest in crystal-embroidered organza gowns and dresses with origami-like folds.

Chanel transformed the Grand Palais into a sunlit Mediterranean garden for its spring couture show, complete with a colonnade of cypress trees and urns of pink roses. Flowers were a theme for the looks, too, which came in two main silhouettes: long and lean — like a smocked black column dress — or bouffant, like a series of pastel party dresses with tiered bell-shaped skirts stitched with intricate lace blossoms.

Monday, Jan. 21

Fans of Giambattistta Valli have come to expect drama from the designer’s couture shows — and his spring 2019 show in Paris didn’t disappoint. There were tiered tulle dresses with voluminous trains, tight cocktail dresses topped with black velvet bows, and fez-like hats. The message was feminine and playful — with a distinctly ’80s touch.

Fashion month, with its traveling shows, often draws comparisons to a circus. At Christian Dior’s spring 2019 couture show, Maria Grazia Chiuri took this idea one step further, presenting looks inspired by harlequins and the big top. Models wore dramatic tulle ruffs, tailored ringmaster jackets, sheer organza shirts with billowing balloon sleeves and eyeliner that evoked Pierrot the clown. There was even a troupe of female acrobats who formed a human arch above the runway.

Always a couture week standout, the designer Iris van Herpen found inspiration in celestial maps and the different portrayals of the feminine form in mythology for her “Shift Souls” collection. That translated to textured gowns laser-cut into unlikely, billowing shapes; van Herpen also invited the artist Kim Keever to contribute a fluffy cloud print that became the base of the most stunning looks.

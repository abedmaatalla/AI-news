    Globetrotting

    JAN. 19, 2019

    Your sneak preview of books coming out in 2019 from around the world.

    Thoka Maer

    Globetrotting

    Your sneak preview of books coming out in 2019 from around the world.
    Show All
    Africa
    Asia
    Europe
    Latin America & the Caribbean
    Middle East
    Denmark
    A Change of Time
    A schoolteacher rebuilds her identity after her husband, the town doctor, dies.
    By Ida Jessen. Translated from the Danish by Martin Aitken. Available April 9 from Archipelago.
    Austria
    Abel and Cain
    First published in 1985, Rezzori’s “The Death of My Brother Abel” is released this year with a posthumously published prequel.
    By Gregor Von Rezzori. Translated from the German by David Dollenmayer, Joachim Neugroschel and Marshall Yarbrough. Available April 9 from New York Review Books.
    Sweden
    Acts of Infidelity
    A young woman who begins an affair with an actor comes to terms with her role as a mistress.
    By Lena Andersson. Translated from the Swedish by Saskia Vogel. Available April 23 from Other Press.
    France
    All Happy Families
    Le Tellier, a prominent French writer, meditates on life and dysfunctional family relationships.
    By Hervé Le Tellier. Translated from the French by Adriana Hunter. Available March 26 from Other Press.
    Argentina
    All My Goodbyes
    A self-critical and self-punishing Argentine woman settles in the southernmost region of Patagonia only to be caught up in horrific murders.
    By Mariana Dimópulos. Translated from the Spanish by Alice Whitmore. Available Feb. 5 from Transit Books.
    Switzerland
    Allmen and the Pink Diamond
    Johann Friedrich von Allmen and his Guatemalan butler, Carlos, a detective duo, search for a missing pink diamond.
    By Martin Suter. Translated from the German by Steph Morris. Available March 5 from New Vessel Press.
    Sweden
    Any Means Necessary
    In this sequel to “Leona: The Die Is Cast,” a corrupt detective grapples with the fallout from her actions and investigates a terrorist attack in Stockholm.
    By Jenny Rogneby. Translated from the Swedish by Agnes Broomé. Available Feb. 12 from Other Press.
    Thailand
    Arid Dreams
    Thirteen stories that explore race, gender and disillusionment in working-class Thailand.
    By Duanwad Pimwana. Translated from the Thai by Mui Poopoksakul. Available April 16 from Feminist Press.
    France
    Article 353
    A noir novel set in coastal France about the murder of a real estate developer.
    By Tanguy Viel. Translated from the French by William Rodarmor. Available March 12 from Other Press.
    Italy
    At the Wolf’s Table
    A young woman flees war-torn Berlin for the countryside only to be recruited as one of Hitler’s tasters.
    By Rosella Postorino. Translated from the Italian by Leah Janeczko. Available Jan. 29 from Flatiron.
    France
    Bakhita
    A novel about a slave who becomes a saint, inspired by a true story.
    By Véronique Olmi. Translated from the French by Adriana Hunter. Available April 16 from Other Press.
    Argentina
    Birthday
    Meditations on turning 50 years old from one of Argentina’s most celebrated writers.
    By César Aira. Translated from the Spanish by Chris Andrews. Available Feb. 28 from New Directions.
    South Africa
    Bom Boy
    Leke — a troubled young man living in Cape Town — tries to break a family curse.
    By Yewande Omotoso. Available Jan. 20 from Catalyst Press.
    Finland
    Crossing
    Two friends leave their homes in communist Albania behind in search of new identities and lives in Italy.
    By Pajtim Statovci. Translated from the Finnish by David Hackston. Available April 2 from Pantheon.
    Argentina
    Dance for Me When I Die
    The story of Víctor Manuel Vital (also known as Frente), a Robin Hood-style legend in Argentina, who was fatally shot by Buenos Aires police officers in 1999.
    By Cristian Alarcón. Translated from the Spanish by Nick Caistor and Marcela López Levy. Available April 5 from Duke University Pres.
    Syria
    Death Is Hard Work
    Estranged siblings set aside their differences to travel through war-torn Syria and fulfill their father’s dying wish.
    By Khaled Khalifa. Translated from the Arabic by Leri Price. Available Feb. 12 from Farrar, Straus & Giroux.
    Croatia
    EEG
    In Drndic’s final novel, published posthumously, a retired pyschologist dissects society and his environment after a failed suicide attempt.
    By Dasa Drndic. Translated from the Croatian by Celia Hawkesworth. Available April 30 from New Directions.
    Greece
    Good Will Come From the Sea
    Four loosely connected stories about the Greek economic crisis.
    By Christos Ikonomou. Translated from the Greek by Karen Emmerich. Available Feb. 26 from Archipelago.
    Norway
    Henrik Ibsen: The Man and the Mask
    A new biography of one of the most important playwrights of the 19th century.
    By Ivo de Figueiredo. Translated from the Norwegian by Robert Ferguson. Available April 2 from Yale University Press.
    France
    Hold Fast Your Crown
    A writer obsessed with American cinema embarks on a journey that takes him from New York to a lake in Italy.
    By Yannick Haenel. Translated from the French by Teresa Fagan. Available April 2 from Other Press.
    Zimbabwe
    House of Stone
    A debut novel that chronicles the fall of Rhodesia through the early days of Zimbabwe.
    By Novuyo Rosa Tshuma. Available Jan. 29 from Norton.
    Colombia
    I’m Not Here to Give a Speech
    A collection of the speeches of the Nobel Prize-winning author who died in 2014, now available for the first time in English.
    By Gabriel García Márquez. Translated from the Spanish by Edith Grossman. Available Jan. 8 from Vintage.
    Iran
    I’m Writing You From Tehran
    A journalist returns to her family home in Iran and chronicles the country’s changes over the course of a decade.
    By Delphine Minoui. Translated from the French by Emma Ramadan. Available April 2 from Farrar, Straus & Giroux.
    Austria
    Intimate Ties
    Originally published in 1911, these two novellas involve a young woman in the throes of sexual and romantic troubles.
    By Robert Musil. Translated from the German by Peter Wortsman. Available April 2 from Archipelago.
    Greenland
    Last Night in Nuuk
    A debut novel about five young Greenlanders on the cusp of adulthood.
    By Niviaq Korneliussen. Translated from the Danish by Anna Halager. Available Jan. 15 from Grove Atlantic.
    Spain
    Mac’s Problem
    Strange things begin to happen in the life of a 60-year-old man who decides to edit his neighbor’s stories, most of which are narrated by a ventriloquist who has lost the ability to speak in different voices.
    By Enrique Vila-Matas. Translated from the Spanish by Margaret Jull Costa and Sophie Hughes. Available April 30 from New Directions.
    Bosnia
    Mars
    A debut story collection of dark speculative fiction.
    By Asja Bakic. Translated from the Croatian by Jennifer Zoble. Available March 19 from Feminist Press.
    Argentina
    Mouthful of Birds
    A short story collection filled with unsettling tales from the author of “Fever Dream.”
    By Samanta Schweblin. Translated from the Spanish by Megan McDowell. Available Jan. 8 from Riverhead. Read our review.
    France
    My Friends
    A clown roams the streets of Paris in search of companionship.
    By Emmanuel Bove. Translated from the French by Janet Louth. Available April 16 from New York Review Books.
    France
    Now, Now, Louison
    A portrait of Louise Bourgeois, the prolific 20th-century French artist.
    By Jean Frémon. Translated from the French by Cole Swensen. Available March 29 from New Directions.
    Argentina
    Optic Nerve
    The story of an Argentine woman told through her relationship with various artists and their works.
    By María Gainza. Translated from the Spanish by Thomas Bunstead. Available April 9 from Catapult.
    South Korea
    Princess Bari
    The story of a young girl who escapes famine and death in 1990s North Korea.
    By Hwang Sok-yong. Translated from the Korean by Sora Kim-Russell. Available April 16 from Scribe.
    Mozambique
    Rain
    Published in the aftermath of Mozambique’s civil war, this collection grapples with what’s been lost and the country’s future.
    By Mia Couto. Translated from the Portuguese by Eric M. B. Becker. Available Feb. 19 from Biblioasis.
    India
    River of Fire
    Considered the most important work of 20th-century Urdu fiction, this book follows four central characters over the course of two millenniums in India.
    By Qurratulain Hyder. Translated from the Urdu by the author. Available March 29 from New Directions.
    Russia
    Rock, Paper, Scissors
    Twelve short stories from a contemporary Russian master of the form, translated into English for the first time.
    By Maxim Osipov. Translated from the Russian by Boris Dralyuk, Alex Fleming and Anne Marie Jackson. Available April 9 from New York Review Books.
    Syria
    Sentence to Hope
    The first English-language collection of plays and essays by one of the Arab world’s most significant writers of the 20th century.
    By Sa’dallah Wannous. Translated from the Arabic by Robert Myers and Nada Saab. Available March 26 from Yale University Press.
    Italy
    Snapshots
    From the author of “Danube” comes a collection of intimate meditations on nearly two decades of life and culture.
    By Claudio Magris. Translated from the Italian by Anne Milano Appel. Available Feb. 26 from Yale University Press.
    Syria
    Songs of Mihyar the Damascene
    A new translation of Adonis’s poetry written while he was on a scholarship in Paris from 1960 to 1961.
    By Adonis. Translated from the Arabic by Kareem James Abu-Zeid and Ivan Eubanks. Available April 30 from New Directions.
    Uruguay
    Springtime in a Broken Mirror
    The story of a political prisoner jailed after a military coup and his family's adjustment to life in exile.
    By Mario Benedetti. Translated from the Spanish by Nick Caistor. Available April 30 from The New Press.
    Japan
    Star
    Mishima, who was an actor himself, paints a pyschological portrait of a celebrity slowly unraveling.
    By Yukio Mishima. Translated from the Japanese by Sam Bett. Available April 30 from New Directions.
    Dominican Republic
    Tentacle
    A young maid from Santo Domingo discovers that she must travel back in time to save the world.
    By Rita Indiana. Translated from the Spanish by Achy Obejas. Available Jan. 15 from And Other Stories.
    Japan
    Territory of Light
    A novel that chronicles a young woman’s attempt to restart her life in Tokyo.
    By Yuko Tsushima. Translated from the Japanese by Geraldine Harcourt. Available Feb. 12 from Farrar, Straus & Giroux.
    Brazil
    The Besieged City
    The third novel from the Brazilian modernist writer about a young woman and the growth of her small town.
    By Clarice Lispector. Translated from the Portuguese by Johnny Lorenz. Available April 30 from New Directions.
    France
    The Cook
    A coming-of-age story centered around a young, self-taught cook as he travels through different cities.
    By Maylis de Kerangal. Translated from the French by Sam Taylor. Available March 26 from Farrar, Straus & Giroux.
    Russia
    The Coronation
    The latest from one of Russia’s most celebrated crime fiction writers is a tale set on the eve of Czar Nicholas II’s coronation.
    By Boris Akunin. Translated from the Russian by Andrew Bromfield. Available Feb. 5 from Grove Atlantic.
    Kenya
    The Dragonfly Sea
    A young woman leaves her home on an island off the coast of Kenya for the Far East where she discovers friends, enemies and love.
    By Yvonne Adhiambo Owuor. Available March 12 from Knopf.
    Germany
    The End of Loneliness
    After the sudden death of their parents, three siblings struggle to recover the family they once were.
    By Benedict Wells. Translated from the German by Charlotte Collins. Available Jan. 29 from Penguin Books.
    Germany
    The Fox and Dr. Shimamura
    A young Japanese medical student is sent to the provinces to cure women of an epidemic of “fox possessions.”
    By Christine Wunnicke. Translated from the German by Philip Boehm. Available April 30 from New Directions.
    Russia
    The Goose Fritz
    A young Russian man delves into his family’s German past.
    By Sergei Lebedev. Translated from the Russian by Antonina W. Bouis. Available March 19 from New Vessel Press.
    Mexico
    The House of the Pain of Others
    This mix of reportage, essay and personal reflection attempts to reckon with the 1911 massacre of 300 Chinese immigrants in Mexico.
    By Julián Herbert. Translated from the Spanish by Christina MacSweeney. Available April 16 from Graywolf.
    Ghana
    The Hundred Wells of Salaga
    Based on true events, this is a story about love and courage in precolonial Ghana.
    By Ayesha Harruna Attah. Available Feb. 5 from Other Press.
    Mexico
    The Murmur of Bees
    A mysterious child with the power to see into the future protects his adoptive family.
    By Sofía Segovia. Translated from the Spanish by Simon Bruni. Available April 16 from AmazonCrossing.
    South Korea
    The Nine Cloud Dream
    A new translation of a Korean literary masterpiece about a young monk trying to comprehend the fundamental truths of the Buddha’s teachings.
    By Kim Man-Jung. Translated from the Korean by Heinz Insu Fenkl. Available Feb. 12 from Penguin Classics.
    Spain
    The Nocilla Trilogy
    The first complete English translation of a landmark trilogy in contemporary Spanish literature.
    By Agustín Fernández Mallo. Translated from the Spanish by Thomas Bunstead. Available Feb. 19 from Farrar, Straus & Giroux.
    Sweden
    The Polyglot Lovers
    Ellinor, a smart and unsentimental woman, gets stranded by a snowstorm with a literary critic after trying online dating.
    By Lina Wolff. Translated from the Swedish by Saskia Vogel. Available April 2 from And Other Stories.
    Netherlands
    The Republic
    The friend of a dead pop philosopher impersonates him at a symposium for historians in Vienna.
    By Joost de Vries. Translated from the Dutch by Jane Hedley-Prole. Available April 30 from Other Press.
    Chile
    The Spirit of Science Fiction
    The story of two young poets in Mexico City trying to make it in the literary world.
    By Roberto Bolaño. Translated from the Spanish by Natasha Wimmer. Available Feb. 5 from Penguin Press.
    Italy
    The Unnamable Present
    A meditation on society's transformation in the 21st century.
    By Roberto Calasso. Translated from the Italian by Richard Dixon. Available April 9 from Farrar, Straus & Giroux.
    South Korea
    The White Book
    An unnamed narrator struggles to come to terms with how the loss of her older sister, who died as an infant, continues to haunt her family. Shortlisted for the 2018 Man Booker International Prize.
    By Han Kang. Translated from the Korean by Deborah Smith. Available Feb. 19 from Hogarth.
    France
    Waiting for Bojangles
    A young boy and his father try to keep his mentally ill mother happy and safe.
    By Olivier Bourdeaut. Translated from the French by Regan Kramer. Available March 19 from Simon & Schuster.
    France
    Wanderer
    A debut novel about the relationship between a music teacher and his student.
    By Sarah Léon. Translated from the French by John Cullen. Available Jan. 8 from Other Press.
    Ukraine
    What We Live for, What We Die for
    Selected poems exploring the cultural landscape of post-Soviet devastation.
    By Serhiy Zhadan. Translated from the Ukranian by Virlana Tkacz and Wanda Phipps. Available April 16 from Yale University Press.
    Portugal
    What’s in a Name?
    Spare poems — in the style of Emily Dickinson — that interweave the everyday with the dreamlike.
    By Ana Luísa Amaral. Translated from the Portuguese by Margaret Jull Costa. Available March 5 from New Directions.
    France
    Who Killed My Father
    From the author of “The End of Eddy,” a nonfiction work about France’s working class and poor citizens.
    By Édouard Louis. Translated from the French by Lorin Stein. Available March 29 from New Directions.
    Romania
    Women
    A young man from Romania reflects on his relationships with three different women.
    By Mihail Sebastian. Translated from the Romanian by Philip Ó Ceallaigh. Available March 5 from Other Press.
    Produced by Matthew Dorfman, Lovia Gyarkye and Rebecca Lieberman.

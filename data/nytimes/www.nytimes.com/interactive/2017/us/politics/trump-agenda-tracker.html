    Tracking Trump’s Agenda, Step by Step

    By JOSH KELLER, ADAM PEARCE and WILSON ANDREWS UPDATED OCT. 1, 2018

    In his first year in office, President Trump has accomplished some of his major goals, including a sweeping tax overhaul and rolling back Obama-era regulations, and has yet to make significant progress on others, like repealing Obamacare and investing in American infrastructure.

    Possible paths for the administration’s agenda are described below, though other options exist. We’ll update this page as statuses change and new proposals emerge.

    Renegotiate Nafta
    Overhauling trade deals has been one of Mr. Trump’s top priorities as president and he has derided Nafta for years.
    Reach new deal
    Ratification by Congress
    Build a border wall and get Mexico to pay for it
    Mr. Trump ordered the construction of a wall, but he will need new legislation to pay for it. Democrats have shown some willingness to provide wall funding in exchange for a deal to protect beneficiaries of the Obama-era Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals program, but a broad immigration deal remains elusive.

    House approval
    Senate approval

    Build wall
    Mexico pays for it
    Renegotiate the Iran deal
    Mr. Trump withdrew from Iran nuclear deal, leaving the 2015 agreement reached by seven countries in flux. The other six countries, which include Iran, Britain and France, have either said they will remain or are likely to remain in the deal.
    Reimpose sanctions
    Negotiate new deal
    Suspend immigration from what Mr. Trump calls ‘terror-prone regions’
    After key sections of an executive order were blocked by courts, Mr. Trump signed a new order that blocked travel from six predominantly Muslim countries. The new order was also blocked, and the Supreme Court upheld the order.

    Repeal and replace the Affordable Care Act
    The Senate failed to pass several amendments that would have repealed Obamacare after the House passed legislation to repeal and replace major parts of the health law. The Trump Administration continues to take action that could weaken President Obama’s signature law.

    Increase deportations of undocumented immigrants
    Mr. Trump has called on the Department of Homeland Security to expel undocumented immigrants more aggressively. The department reported a drop in border apprehensions and increase in interior arrests in 2017.

    Impose import taxes
    Mr. Trump supports a 20 percent tax on all imports and has considered proposing such a tax on Mexican imports to pay for a border wall. Republicans dropped the proposal from their tax reform plans in 2017.
